What to expect when you arrive

Whether you’re bringing your child for crisis care or arriving for outpatient services yourself, here’s what the process looks like.

For families & youth

1

A warm welcome

A trained staff member meets you and your child at the door. We move at your pace — there’s no rush, and we’ll answer every question before anything happens.

2

Getting to know your child

A calm intake conversation — with you and your child together — helps us build a personalized care plan. Parent and guardian input is essential.

3

The stay begins

Your child will have their own space, daily therapeutic programming, and staff available around the clock. You’ll receive regular updates and clear ways to stay connected.

For adults (outpatient services)

1

You check in

A staff member welcomes you and gets your information. The process is straightforward and confidential.

2

We review your needs

A brief intake conversation helps us understand your goals and match you with the right outpatient program.

3

Your care plan begins

You’ll work with our team to build an individualized plan around your schedule and recovery goals.

What to bring for your child’s stay

Bring this

  • Min. 5 days of medication in original labeled bottles with accurate prescription details
  • Insurance card
  • Comfortable clothing — no more than 5 days’ worth
  • Phone and charger (if applicable for the child’s age)
  • Comfort items from home (stuffed animal, blanket — staff discretion)
  • ER or hospital discharge paperwork (if applicable)
  • Over-the-counter medications (permitted)
  • Essential medical devices

Leave this at home

  • Expired, loose, or unlabeled medication
  • Weapons or sharp objects
  • Alcohol or illegal substances
  • Drug paraphernalia
  • Items that could be unsafe in a shared environment

Not sure what to bring? Call us before arrival — we’re happy to help you prepare. 515-642-7070
