## Short descriptions

- **Crisis Stabilization – Adults.** Inpatient-style short-term stabilization for adults in mental health crisis. Available at Woodward and Perry.
- **Crisis Stabilization – Children.** Same model, ages 5–17. Distinct facility/process from adult stabilization.
- **Transitional Living.** Longer-stay support for people stepping down from stabilization or other intensive services.
- **Peer Support Services.** Support delivered by certified peers — people with lived experience of mental health recovery.
- **Community Support Program.** In-home, ongoing mental health support that meets adults where they are — see [community-support-program](../services/community-support-program.md) for full draft.
- **Outpatient Substance Use Services.** Outpatient treatment for substance use that fits around work and life; sits alongside (not inside) the crisis services line — see [outpatient-substance-use](../services/outpatient-substance-use.md) for full draft.
- **Mobile Crisis Response.** Sends a trained mental-health team to the person in crisis rather than requiring them to travel. Critical for rural Iowa coverage — see [mobile-crisis-response](../services/mobile-crisis-response.md) for full draft.
- **CIT Program (Crisis Intervention Team co-responder).** Pairs an Inside Out mental health professional with a CIT-trained officer on 911 mental-health calls — see [cit-program](../services/cit-program.md) for full draft.
- **Outpatient Therapy.** Ongoing, scheduled one-on-one counseling with a licensed therapist for general mental health (anxiety, depression, grief, trauma) — the non-crisis, everyday-care tier.
