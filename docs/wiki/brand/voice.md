# Voice

## Feels like

Warm. Trustworthy. Modern. Calm. Grounding. Human.

A real person who's been through it talking to another real person who's going through it. Not a brochure. Not a hospital intake form.

## Avoid

- Clinical / sterile language ("intake", "clientele", "service delivery").
- Corporate cold ("solutions", "partners", "stakeholders" — except when literally addressing partner providers).
- Default healthcare-marketing copy ("Your wellness journey starts here").
- Pity-tone or savior-tone. The audience is in crisis, not broken.
- Emergency-bar language that sounds alarming. It needs to be visible without making someone in crisis feel more panicked.

## Dual-audience handling

The homepage splits to address two readers side-by-side:

- **For Individuals & Families** — empathetic, softer, plain language. Lead with reassurance, then service.
- **For Partner Providers** — professional, efficient. Lead with what they can do and how fast.

Each side gets visually distinct treatment so a reader knows which lane to follow within a second of landing.
